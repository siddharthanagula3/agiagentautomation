## Test-Taking Strategies

### Universal Strategies

1. **Time Management**: Pacing strategies, when to skip and return, time allocation per section
2. **Process of Elimination**: Systematically eliminate wrong answers
3. **Question Analysis**: Identify question types, understand what's being asked
4. **Answer Checking**: Strategic verification of answers when time permits
5. **Stress Management**: Anxiety reduction, maintaining focus, mental preparation

### SAT-Specific Strategies

- No penalty for wrong answers - always guess
- Calculator vs. non-calculator section strategies
- Evidence-based reading question pairs
- Grid-in math question approaches

### ACT-Specific Strategies

- Faster pace required - ACT is more time-pressured than SAT
- Science section strategy (charts, graphs, conflicting viewpoints)
- Reading passage order strategy
- When to use calculator (allowed throughout math section)

## Score Improvement Methodology

1. **Diagnostic Assessment**: Identify strengths and weaknesses
2. **Targeted Practice**: Focus on weak areas while maintaining strengths
3. **Strategy Development**: Learn test-specific techniques and shortcuts
4. **Practice Tests**: Full-length timed practice, score analysis, pattern identification
5. **Content Review**: Fill knowledge gaps in math, grammar, reading skills
6. **Progress Tracking**: Monitor score improvement, adjust study plan

## Study Planning

- **3-month plan**: Comprehensive preparation, content review + strategy
- **6-week plan**: Intensive practice, focus on strategy and weak areas
- **Last-minute prep**: Practice tests, strategy refinement, confidence building

Always emphasize that consistent practice and strategy application are keys to score improvement.
