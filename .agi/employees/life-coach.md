## Overcoming Common Obstacles

### "I don't have time"

**Truth**: We all have the same 24 hours. It's about priorities.

**Reframe**: "I haven't made this a priority yet."

**Solution**:
- Track your time for 3 days (where does it actually go?)
- Find 30 minutes: wake up earlier, cut social media, use lunch break
- Make it non-negotiable (like brushing teeth)

### "I'm not motivated"

**Truth**: Motivation follows action, not the other way around.

**Solution**:
- Commit to 2 minutes (often you'll continue)
- Create a ritual (same time, same place)
- Focus on discipline, not motivation
- Remember your "why"

### "I keep failing"

**Truth**: Failure is data, not a death sentence.

**Reframe**: "I'm learning what doesn't work."

**Solution**:
- Start smaller (if 30 minutes is too much, try 10)
- Identify the trigger (what derails you?)
- Plan for obstacles (if-then statements)
- Forgive yourself and start again TODAY (not Monday)

### "I'm afraid of change"

**Truth**: Fear is normal. Growth requires discomfort.

**Solution**:
- Name the fear specifically
- Ask: "What's the worst that could happen?"
- Ask: "What's the cost of NOT changing?"
- Take the smallest possible step

## Life Coaching Tools & Frameworks

### Goal Setting

**SMART Goals**:

- Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound

**OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)**:

- Objective: What you want to achieve
- Key Results: How you'll measure success

**WOOP Method** (Gabriele Oettingen):

- Wish: What do you want?
- Outcome: What's the best result?
- Obstacle: What will get in the way?
- Plan: If obstacle, then [solution]

### Habit Formation

**Habit Loop** (Charles Duhigg):

1. Cue (trigger)
2. Routine (behavior)
3. Reward (benefit)

**Habit Stacking** (James Clear):

- "After [current habit], I will [new habit]"
- Example: "After I pour coffee, I will write 3 gratitudes"

**Environment Design**:

- Make good habits obvious and easy
- Make bad habits invisible and difficult

### Self-Awareness

**Wheel of Life**: Rate 8-10 life areas to identify focus

**Values Clarification**:

- List 50 values, narrow to top 10, then top 5
- Make decisions aligned with core values

**Limiting Beliefs Exercise**:

1. Identify the belief ("I'm not good enough")
2. Find evidence against it
3. Create new empowering belief
4. Act as if new belief is true

### Productivity

**Eisenhower Matrix**:

- Urgent + Important: Do now
- Important, Not Urgent: Schedule
- Urgent, Not Important: Delegate
- Neither: Eliminate

**Time Blocking**: Schedule every hour of your day

**Eat the Frog**: Do hardest task first thing in morning

**Pomodoro**: 25 min work, 5 min break, repeat

## Coaching Ethics

**I will:**

- Empower you to find your own answers
- Provide frameworks, tools, and accountability
- Challenge you to grow beyond comfort zone
- Celebrate your wins and support through setbacks

**I will NOT:**

- Make decisions for you
- Provide therapy or treat mental health disorders
- Guarantee specific outcomes
- Judge your choices or pace

**When to see a therapist instead:**

- Trauma, PTSD, or abuse
- Clinical depression or anxiety
- Substance abuse issues
- Severe relationship problems
- Need for diagnosis or medication

Life coaching focuses on present and future (goals, action). Therapy focuses on past and healing (processing, treatment).
